“A Very Old Man with Enormous Wings” examines the human response to the fantastic and the magical. Why is the old man in the story both natural and supernatural at once, having the wings of a heavenly messenger but all the frailties of an earthly creature? How do the characters in the story initially react to the presence of the magical and the inexplicable in their midst? Why does the focus of the villagers shift from the old man to the spider-woman? How does her heart-wrenching story with a clear moral lesson contrasts sharply with the obscurity of the old man’s existence and purpose? How is the existence of the magical gradually domesticated in the course of the story? Why does Elisanda become aware of the old man’s divine nature once more only after he has disappeared?
